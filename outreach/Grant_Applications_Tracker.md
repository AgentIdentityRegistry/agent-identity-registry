## Key Narrative Points (Use Across All Applications)

1. **The problem is urgent.** NIST, IETF, W3C, and enterprise vendors are actively standardizing agent identity in 2026. Without a neutral nonprofit voice, these standards will be shaped exclusively by corporate interests.

2. **AIR is the only independent option.** 17+ organizations work on agent identity; AIR is the only one governed as a public-interest nonprofit with zero corporate board influence.

3. **We have substance, not just a pitch.** Published specification, governance charter, trust methodology, working demo, NIST public comment submitted, and active standards body engagement.

4. **Open standards foundation.** Built on W3C DIDs and VCs — not proprietary technology, not blockchain-dependent, not locked to any vendor.

5. **Clear path from grant to sustainability.** Grant funding enables production launch; long-term sustainability through diversified revenue (verification fees, membership, certification programs).
