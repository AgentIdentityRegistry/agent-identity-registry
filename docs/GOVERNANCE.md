## Overview

The Agent Identity Registry Foundation is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit corporation dedicated to building neutral, transparent infrastructure for AI agent identity and trust. Our governance structure ensures:

- **Neutrality**: No single corporate or national interest dominates
- **Transparency**: Public decision-making and accountability
- **Inclusivity**: Stakeholder representation across ecosystem
- **Stability**: Clear succession planning and governance continuity
- **Accountability**: To the public interest, not commercial entities

### Committees

#### Technical Steering Committee (TSC)

**Composition**: 5-7 voting members elected by community, 1 nonvoting board liaison  
**Term**: 2 years, staggered  
**Responsibilities**:
- Review and approve technical specifications
- Oversee API and protocol development
- Manage security vulnerability disclosure
- Approve major architectural changes
- Quarterly technical reports to board

#### Trust & Verification Committee (TVC)

**Composition**: 5-7 experts in verification, trust, risk management  
**Term**: 2 years  
**Responsibilities**:
- Oversee trust score methodology
- Certify verifiers
- Handle disputes and appeals
- Annual methodology audit
- Research and improvement recommendations

#### Community & Ethics Committee (CEC)

**Composition**: 5-7 community leaders, creators, users  
**Term**: 2 years  
**Responsibilities**:
- Community engagement and feedback
- Code of conduct enforcement
- Accessibility and inclusion
- Public communications
- Annual community survey

## Decision-Making Process

### Types of Decisions

#### 1. Routine Operations (Board Authority)

**Examples**: Staff hiring, budget allocation, vendor contracts  
**Authority**: Executive Director with board oversight  
**Process**: Normal business practices  
**Transparency**: Reported in quarterly board minutes

#### 2. Policy & Governance (Board Approval)

**Examples**: Governance structure changes, major policy shifts  
**Authority**: Board vote required (5+ of 7 members)  
**Voting**: One person, one vote (no weighted voting)  
**Process**:
- 2-week public comment period
- Board discussion and vote
- Public announcement with rationale

#### 3. Technical Standards (TSC + Board Approval)

**Examples**: Specification changes, API versioning, security protocols  
**Authority**: TSC recommendation, board final approval  
**Voting**: 2/3 TSC majority  
**Process**:
- RFC (Request for Comments) period: 4 weeks
- Community discussion on GitHub
- TSC review and vote
- Board approval (majority vote)
- Implementation timeline announced

#### 4. Trust Methodology (TVC + Board Approval)

**Examples**: Trust score algorithm changes, verifier criteria  
**Authority**: TVC recommendation, board approval  
**Voting**: 2/3 TVC majority  
**Process**:
- Community consultation: 6 weeks
- TVC investigation: 4 weeks
- Board review: 2 weeks
- Phased implementation over 3-6 months
- Dispute resolution process published

#### 5. Emergency Decisions (Board Authority)

**Examples**: Security incidents, operational emergencies  
**Authority**: Board + Executive Director (expedited)  
**Voting**: Majority vote with 24-hour notice where possible  
**Process**:
- Immediate action to mitigate risk
- Within 48 hours: full board notification
- Retroactive approval at next board meeting
- Public transparency report within 7 days

### Voting Principles

- **One member, one vote**: No weighted voting based on organization size or funding
- **Supermajority for major changes**: 2/3 majority required for technical changes
- **Transparency**: All votes recorded and published (with recusal noted)
- **Recusal**: Members abstain on conflicts of interest (formal disclosure)
- **Remote voting**: Electronic voting permitted with documented proof

### Amendment Process

Changes to governance structure require:

1. **Proposal**: Written proposal with rationale
2. **Comment period**: 8 weeks public comment
3. **Board discussion**: Two separate board meetings
4. **Vote**: 5 of 7 board members must approve
5. **Documentation**: Amendment recorded with effective date
6. **Communication**: Public announcement and education period

## Transparency Commitments

### Public Information

The Foundation publishes:

**Quarterly**:
- Board meeting minutes (redacted for privacy/legal)
- Technical Steering Committee decisions
- Financial statements (nonprofit filing)
- Community metrics (agents registered, transactions, etc.)

**Annually**:
- Detailed annual report

- Roadmap for next 18 months

**On-Going**:
- RFC and comment periods (public GitHub)

- Conflict of interest disclosures (summary level)

- Key documents translated to major languages
- Board meetings livestreamed (where confidentiality permits)

## Financial Management

### Funding Model

**Revenue Sources**:
- Foundation grants and donations
- Enterprise API licensing (modest fees)
- Professional services and consulting (related to AIR)
- Government/research grants

**Non-revenue**: No tracking data sales, no sponsored rankings, no proprietary data extraction

### Budget Process

1. **Proposal** (June): Staff proposes budget for next fiscal year
2. **Review** (July): Finance committee reviews and provides feedback
3. **Board approval** (August): Full board votes on budget
4. **Public disclosure** (September): Budget published with rationale
5. **Quarterly reporting**: Actual vs. budget reported each quarter

### Financial Controls

- Independent audit (annual, published)
- Segregation of duties
- Expense approval policies
- Nonprofit compliance (501(c)(3) requirements)
- Board finance committee oversight

### Conflict Resolution

**Donations with strings attached**: Declined unless aligned with mission and disclosed

**Commercialization**: Commercial use of AIR data/API must follow published licensing; proceeds support operations

**Influence attempts**: Documented and reported; transparency about pressure

## Succession Planning

### Board Succession

- Staggered 3-year terms ensure continuity (2-3 directors replaced each year)
- No consecutive term limits; encourage 2-3 terms for stability
- Special procedures for emergency vacancies (appointed until next election)

### Executive Director Succession

- Board maintains updated succession plan (confidential)
- Deputy Director role ensures knowledge transfer
- 6-month transition period for new executive director

### Key Person Dependencies

- Deliberately avoid single points of failure
- Cross-training on critical functions
- Documentation requirements
- Knowledge management system maintained

## Accountability & Oversight

### Internal Oversight

- **Audit committee**: Reviews financial and operational compliance (quarterly)
- **Governance committee**: Reviews governance practices annually
- **Annual self-assessment**: Board evaluates own performance

### External Oversight

- **Independent auditor**: Annual financial audit (published)
- **Charity watchdog compliance**: Meets Guidestar/similar standards
- **Legal review**: Nonprofit counsel on compliance issues
- **Regulatory filings**: IRS Form 990 filed annually (public)

### Remediation Process

If misconduct discovered:

1. Investigation by governance committee
2. Fact-finding and documentation (confidential if legally required)
3. Remediation plan if issues confirmed
4. Transparency report published (with appropriate redactions)
5. Prevention measures implemented

### Whistleblower Protection

- Protected channel: whistleblower@agentidentityregistry.org
- Anonymous submission accepted
- No retaliation for good-faith reports
- Investigation confidentiality (unless public interest override)

## Conflict of Interest

### Policy

Board members and committee leaders must:

1. **Disclose** conflicts of interest annually (confidential)
2. **Recuse** from voting on matters with direct financial interest
3. **Abstain** from sensitive information access if conflicted
4. **Decline** gifts or special treatment from organizations with interests in AIR

### Examples of Conflicts

**Clear conflicts**:
- Direct financial interest in outcome
- Family member affected
- Employment at interested organization
- Recent significant donation

**Managed conflicts**:
- Working in related field (common for board members)
- Passive investment in related companies
- Academic research on related topics

**Resolution**: Case-by-case evaluation; recusal required for voting

## Relationship with Broader Ecosystem

### Standards Bodies

AIR coordinates with:
- W3C (Decentralized Identifiers, Verifiable Credentials)
- NIST (AI Risk Management Framework)

AIR does not attempt to replace these bodies; instead, works within established standards.

### Open Source Community

AIR code uses open-source licenses (Apache 2.0) and contributes to broader ecosystem. Board member(s) engage with related open-source communities.

### Regulatory Engagement

Foundation engages with regulators and policymakers:
- Shares technical expertise
- Participates in policy consultations
- Does not lobby for commercial interests
- Maintains political neutrality
